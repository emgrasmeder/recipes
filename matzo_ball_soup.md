# Matzo ball soup

## Ingredients overview

Use this for shopping; detailed amounts are repeated under each part.

- **Onions:** about **4–5 baseball-sized total** — **2** for the broth, **2–3** for the matzo mixture (sautéed separately).
- **Carrots:** **2** large (not ridiculous).
- **Celery:** **6** stalks.
- **Coconut oil:** for **two** steps — **1–2 generous tablespoons** to sauté the broth vegetables, plus more to **fry the onions** for the matzo mix (don’t hold back on that sauté).
- **Salt:** **2–3 generous tablespoons** for seasoning the **broth** (in the pot); separately, about **1 teaspoon salt** and **3 teaspoons pepper** for the **matzo dough** (not the same salt as “to taste” in the big pot).
- **Water:** about **1–2 liters** to build the broth.
- **Matzos:** **12–15** matzot, smashed or crumbled.
- EITHER
  - **Eggs:** about **5** OR
  - **Potato Starch:** 3 tablespoons
- **Fresh herbs (e.g. Rewe packets):** parsley **~1.7** packets, cilantro **1**, dill **1** — all chopped very fine for the mix.
- **Baking soda:** a scant **1 teaspoon** (less than a teaspoon per the original note).
- **Extra:** **salted water** in a separate pot to boil the matzo balls (not counted in the broth salt above).

The **broth you simmer in part 1** is what you use to moisten the matzo crumbs in part 2 — not a second mystery stock.

---

## Part 1: Broth

### Ingredients

- Onions — **2** baseball-sized, chopped
- Carrots — **2** large, chopped
- Celery — **6** stalks, chopped
- Coconut oil — **1–2** generous tablespoons
- Salt — **2–3** generous tablespoons (for the pot; adjust until it tastes OK)
- Water — about **1–2** liters

### Steps

1. The important part of the broth is onion, carrot, and celery. You can add more things if you want. The coconut oil helps approximate a rich, meaty flavor.
2. In coconut oil, sauté chopped **onions**, then chop the **carrots** and throw them in, then chop the **celery** and throw them in. Stir occasionally and don't let them burn for **15–20 minutes**.
3. Add water and salt until the broth tastes OK.
4. Bring to a boil, then simmer for **a couple of hours**.
5. Add any extras you like.

Reserve enough of this finished broth to dampen the matzo mixture in part 2 (damp, not soggy).

---

## Part 2: Matzo balls

### Ingredients

- Onions — **2–3** baseball-sized, as finely chopped as possible
- Coconut oil — to fry the onion. Again, be generous
- Parsley — 120g
- Cilantro — 80g
- Dill — 80g
- Matzos — **12–15** big squares, smashed up
- Broth from **part 1** — enough to make the matzo damp but not soggy
- Salt — about **1 teaspoon**
- Black pepper — about **3 teaspoons**
- Baking soda — **less than 1 teaspoon**
- Eggs (5) OR Potato Starch (3 tablespoons?)


### Steps

1. Sauté the finely chopped onion in coconut oil for a long time until **brown but not crispy**.
2. In a large bowl, combine very finely chopped herbs, **broth from part 1**, salt and pepper **for the dough**, smashed matzos, baking soda, and the sautéed onion.
3. Aim for roughly **⅔** matzo and **⅓** herbs and stuff by look; adjust to taste.
4. Add the egg/replacement until the mixture is sticky and dough-like.
5. Chill **30+ minutes** in the fridge so the matzo can soak everything up.
6. Form balls **smaller than you think**, like ping-pong balls at biggest — they expand when boiled.
7. Bring some **salted water** to a boil (it should taste almost like the ocean), and plop in balls - they need to boil for **30–45 minutes**.
8. Use right away or freeze.

The water left after cooking the balls is tasty and **can be stirred into the soup broth**.

---

## Serving

Serve gently, the balls are fluffy but a little delicate.
